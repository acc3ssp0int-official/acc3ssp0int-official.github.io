Unit-9

Analysing Qualitative Data

Assignment Formative: Unit-9 Summary Post

The act of deciphering non-numerical data, such as interview transcripts, observations, or documents, to find underlying patterns and meanings is known as qualitative data analysis (Braun & Clarke, 2006). Coding, theme analysis, and discourse analysis are methods. The method gives comprehensive comprehension of context, social processes, and human viewpoints in addition to rich, nuanced insights into complicated events (Creswell & Poth, 2017). To guarantee reliability, it might take some time and needs expert interpretation.



References
  • Braun, V., & Clarke, V. (2006). Using thematic analysis in psychology. Qualitative Research in Psychology, 3(2), 77-101.
  • Creswell, J. W., & Poth, C. N. (2017). Qualitative inquiry and research design: Choosing among five approaches. Sage Publications.
