Unit-7

Validity and Generalisability in Research

Assignment Summative: Unit-7 Literature Review
Tutor Feedback Summative: Unit-7 Literature Review
Assignment Formative: Unit-7 Initial Post

Robust research must have essential components including dependability, generalizability, and validity. The correctness and veracity of study results are referred to as validity (Bryman, 2012). The capacity of study results to be applied to many contexts or people is known as generalizability (Babbie, 2016). The consistency or reproducibility of study findings is referred to as reliability (Neuman, 2013). They raise the credibility of research when combined. High reliability guarantees that research results may be repeated over time and by other researchers. High validity assures that research measures what it intended to measure.

References

  • Bryman, A. (2012). Social research methods. Oxford University Press.
  • Babbie, E. (2016). The practice of social research. Cengage Learning.
  • Neuman, W. L. (2013). Social research methods: Qualitative and quantitative approaches. Pearson.
