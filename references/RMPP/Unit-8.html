Inferential Statistics

Assignment Formative: Unit-8 Peer Response 1
Assignment Formative: Unit-8 Peer Response 2
Assignment Formative: Unit-8 Peer Response 3

According to Field (2013), statistical inference is the act of using data from a sample to make inferences about a population. To account for random variability in the data, statistical models are used, along with estimation and hypothesis testing (Casella & Berger, 2017). It is crucial to research because it enables informed decision-making and theory testing, allowing researchers to generate hypotheses, make predictions, and identify links (Hays, 1994). However, the sampling strategy and suitability of the used statistical models determine its validity.

References

  • Field, A. (2013). Discovering statistics using IBM SPSS statistics. Sage Publications.
  • Casella, G., & Berger, R. L. (2017). Statistical inference. Cengage Learning.
  • Hays, W. L. (1994). Statistics. Harcourt Brace College Publishers
