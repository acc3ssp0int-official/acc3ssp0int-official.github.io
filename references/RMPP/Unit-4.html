Unit-4

Case Studies, Focus Groups and Observations

Assignment Formative: Unit-4 Literature Outline
Tutor Feedback Formative: Unit-4 Literature Outline

A focus group is a qualitative research technique that entails a controlled conversation on a specific subject among a small number of participants (Morgan, 1996). A researcher facilitates it with the intention of eliciting various viewpoints, responses, or experiences relevant to the study issue. Focus groups are useful for doing exploratory research, formulating hypotheses, and acquiring in-depth understanding of participant attitudes and behaviours. They may not be representative of the whole population and are prone to groupthink (Stewart & Shamdasani, 2014).
