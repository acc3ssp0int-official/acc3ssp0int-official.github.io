Unit-5

Interviews and Survey Methods

A qualitative research technique known as an in-depth interview involves one-on-one discussions between a researcher and a participant with the goal of thoroughly examining complicated phenomena or unique experiences (DiCicco-Bloom & Crabtree, 2006). They enable in-depth examination of a participant's ideas, emotions, and experiences, offering rich, nuanced insights that other approaches could overlook Ritchie, J. et al. (2014). They also provide flexibility for follow-up or in-depth inquiries, enriching and deepening the data. They may, however, take a lot of time and need expert interviews and analysis.

References

  • DiCicco‐Bloom, B., & Crabtree, B. F. (2006). The qualitative research interview. Medical Education, 40(4), 314-321.
  • Ritchie, J. et al. (2014) Qualitative research practice : a guide for social science students and researchers. Second edition / [edited by] Jane Ritchie, Jane Lewis, Carol McNaughton Nicholls, Rachel Ormston. Jane Ritchie et al. (eds.). Los Angeles: SAGE.
