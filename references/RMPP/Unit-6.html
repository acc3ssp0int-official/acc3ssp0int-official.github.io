Unit-6

Questionnaire Designs

A crucial step in quantitative research that involves the development of organised instruments for data collecting is questionnaire design (Bowling, 2005). In order to facilitate comparisons and statistical analysis, well-designed questionnaires allow for the systematic and standardised gathering of data (Fink, 2013). Their effectiveness, capacity to gather data from huge populations, and capacity to provide quantitative and generalizable outcomes make them advantageous (Wetzel, 2010). To guarantee accurate and trustworthy data, questionnaire design must take question clarity, answer alternatives, and possible bias into careful consideration.

References

  • Bowling, A. (2005). Mode of questionnaire administration can have serious effects on data quality. Journal of Public Health, 27(3), 281-291.
  • Wetzel, A.P. (2010). Internet, mail, and mixed-mode surveys: The tailored design method*. Journal of Continuing Education in the Health Professions, 30(3), p.206. doi:https://doi.org/10.1002/chp.20083.
  • Fink, A. (2013). How to design survey studies. Sage Publications.
